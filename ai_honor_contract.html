Purpose

As AI tools become increasingly integrated into education, they provide valuable assistance in brainstorming, refining ideas, and improving clarity in academic work. However, AI should enhance critical thinking, comprehension, and engagement, not replace them.

This contract outlines the acceptable use of AI in this online course and the expectations for submitting AI-assisted work with integrity. Failure to adhere to these guidelines may result in point deductions, mandatory rewrites, or other academic consequences at the instructor's discretion.

Acceptable use of AI in this course

AI tools may be used for:

  • Generating ideas and refining arguments as a starting point, not a final submission.
  • Clarifying complex physiological concepts through discussion and explanation, not copying.
  • Checking for grammar, spelling, and clarity in writing.
  • Providing alternative perspectives to challenge and enhance your understanding.

AI tools may NOT be used for:

  • Submitting AI-generated responses as your own original work.
  • Copying AI-generated answers without critical analysis.
  • Using AI to bypass required readings, discussions, or problem-solving.
  • Relying on AI for case study diagnoses, physiological processes, or critical thinking tasks without verifying accuracy.
  • Generating workbook labels, drawings, or synthesis answers. Lab workbooks must be hand-labeled.
  • Writing discussion posts or replies in your name.
  • Completing Honorlock-proctored quizzes. AI use during a proctored quiz is automatically a violation.

Expectations for AI-generated content submission

To ensure academic integrity, students who use AI in any part of an assignment where AI use is permitted must:

  1. Provide full transparency. Submit a completed AI Transparency Document alongside the assignment. The document lives at ai_transparency_document.html and is also linked from the syllabus resources.
  2. Use Google Docs or another tool with revision history enabled. All written assignments where AI is permitted must be submitted with revision history available so I can see how AI-assisted content was refined and how much effort you put into your own learning. Submitting a static final version without revision history may result in point deductions or a requirement to rewrite.
  3. Demonstrate critical thinking. AI responses frequently contain errors, oversimplifications, or outdated information. If you blindly repeat AI-generated mistakes, it suggests a lack of understanding and will be penalized. Any physiological concepts derived from AI must be critically analyzed and verified before inclusion.
  4. Cite AI assistance properly. If AI helped generate specific ideas, acknowledge it (for example: "ChatGPT assisted in brainstorming this explanation, but final analysis and synthesis were my own"). Do not pass AI-generated content off as solely your own work.

The AI Transparency Document (review these instructions any time)

When an assignment in this course explicitly permits AI use, you must submit a separate AI Transparency Document with your work. The document is a short fillable HTML form you complete, print to PDF, and upload alongside your assignment.

What the AI Transparency Document captures

  • Which AI tool you used (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, or other).
  • A link to the AI conversation (use the share-link feature in your AI tool).
  • Which parts of the assignment AI influenced, in plain language.
  • A 2 to 3 sentence reflection on how AI helped you refine your work and where you applied critical thinking beyond AI's suggestions.
  • A statement that you verified accuracy of any physiological information from AI against the OpenStax textbook or another reliable source.
  • Your typed signature and the date.

Open the fillable transparency document here: ai_transparency_document.html. Fill it in, print to PDF, and upload it as a second file when you submit the AI-permitted assignment.

Default rule: If an assignment does not say AI is permitted, AI is not permitted. Lab workbooks, discussion posts, and proctored quizzes never permit AI.

Consequences for misuse

Violations of this policy are subject to instructor discretion and may include:

  • Point deductions for failing to properly analyze, critique, or refine AI-generated responses.
  • Required rewrites if AI-generated errors are repeated without critical evaluation.
  • Zero credit for work that appears to be entirely AI-generated without student effort.
  • Further academic penalties if violations persist (referral to ARC academic standards process).

Revision penalties

  • If a student is required to revise and resubmit an assignment due to AI misuse, the first revision is penalized 10%.
  • For each additional revision beyond the first, a 25% reduction of points is applied after the rubric grade.
  • If revisions are required multiple times because of continued failure to critically analyze AI-generated content, the assignment may be graded as incomplete or receive a failing grade.
