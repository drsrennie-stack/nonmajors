Welcome to BIO 304. I am genuinely glad you are here. Whether you are heading toward nursing school, EMT or paramedic certification, mortuary science, healthcare interpreting, or another path entirely, the structure and function of the human body is one of the most useful subjects you can ever learn. What a typical week looks like

Weekly rhythm

Each week of BIO 304 follows the same rhythm. The pacing is built so the work distributes evenly across 7 days rather than piling up on the weekend. You will not finish a week's work the night before it is due. Plan for daily engagement.

A typical week at a glance

  • Monday Watch the week's videos. Concept lectures (recorded by Dr. Rennie + curated YouTube content) and lab ID videos. About 6 hours total.
  • Tuesday OpenStax reading. Read assigned chapters from the Reading Map. Run cognitive trainer session 1.
  • Wednesday Initial discussion post due 11:59 PM. Run cognitive trainer session 2. Open the week's portfolio template.
  • Thursday Anatomy workbook. Complete the drawings, labels, tables, and synthesis questions.
  • Friday Physiology workbook. Complete the clinical dry lab, calculations, and case responses. Run cognitive trainer session 3 (optional 4th).
  • Saturday Build the portfolio page using content from the workbooks. Take the quiz (Honorlock-proctored, 30 min, 1 attempt).
  • Sunday Submit everything by 11:59 PM: anatomy workbook (PDF), physiology workbook (PDF), portfolio page (PDF), discussion replies (2). No late work accepted.

This is one possible rhythm. Adjust to your own schedule, but keep daily engagement. The cognitive trainer in particular benefits from spaced practice (3 to 4 short sessions) rather than one long session.

How to get help

Where to ask questions

Most questions in this course should go in the Virtual Office Hours forum on Canvas, not in email. Here is why:

  • Other students benefit. If you have a question, three other students probably have it too. Posting publicly means everyone gets the answer.
  • Faster responses. I check the forum regularly. Email gets answered too, but the forum is my first stop.
  • Searchable record. Questions and answers stay in the forum all semester so you can refer back.
  • Less email traffic. The fewer duplicate questions I answer in email, the more time I have for the harder ones.

Use email or Canvas Inbox for confidential matters only: grade questions, accommodation needs, personal circumstances, anything you would not want classmates to see.

For one-on-one Zoom meetings: email me to schedule. Include your questions in that email so I can prepare resources before we meet. Zoom: https://lrccd.zoom.us/j/4748608065
