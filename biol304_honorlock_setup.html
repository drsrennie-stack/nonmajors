BIO 304 · Before your first proctored quiz

Set up Honorlock. One time, ten minutes.

Some BIO 304 quizzes run in Honorlock, a Chrome extension that locks down your browser during the quiz so you cannot open other tabs, windows, or apps. You install it once, well before quiz day. This page walks you through it and shows you what to do if it acts up.

Important · read this before quiz day

I do not have the Honorlock password. Honorlock does.

If Honorlock will not approve you or let you start, it is almost always because the requirements for a secure exam are not met. The most common causes are more than one browser open, extra tabs, a second monitor, or another device connected. The lockdown browser will not start until your setup is clean, so it will not let you begin.

When that happens, do not email me for a password. There is none for me to give you. Picture it: 11:50 at night, ten minutes left on the clock. I will not be awake to rescue you, and even if you did reach me, I still would not have a password to send.

If you hit a wall, start a live chat with Honorlock right away. They are available 24 hours a day and they are the only ones who can get you in. If chat is down, call 844-243-2500 or email support@honorlock.com.

So do not wait until the last minute. Open your quiz early in the window. That way, if something goes sideways, you still have time to chat with Honorlock and finish before it closes.

What you need

Four things, all required.

Check these first. If any one is missing, Honorlock will not launch on quiz day.

  • Required

    Google Chrome

    Honorlock only runs in Google Chrome. Firefox, Edge, and Safari will not work. Free download at google.com/chrome.

  • Required

    A laptop or desktop

    Windows or Mac. Not a phone, tablet, or Chromebook. Honorlock cannot run on those.

  • Required

    One screen, nothing extra

    A single display with other apps closed. Disconnect any second monitor or extra device. The lockdown will not start if it detects another screen.

  • Required

    Steady internet

    A wired connection or strong Wi-Fi. A weak signal can drop you mid-quiz, so test in the spot where you plan to take it.

Install the extension

Five steps, done once.

Do this a day or two before your first proctored quiz, not five minutes before. You only install the extension one time.

  1. Open Google Chrome

    Use the same laptop or desktop you will test on. If you do not have Chrome yet, install it from google.com/chrome first.

  2. Go to the install page

    In Chrome, visit honorlock.com/install. You can also just open a Honorlock quiz in Canvas, which sends you to the same screen.

  3. Agree, then Get Started

    Check the box agreeing to Honorlock's Terms of Service and Privacy Policy, then click the blue Get Started button.

  4. Add it to Chrome

    Chrome opens the extension page. Click Add to Chrome, then Add extension in the pop-up. Wait for the message confirming the extension was added.

  5. You are set

    A small Honorlock icon now sits in your Chrome toolbar. It stays installed, so you will not repeat this. It only does anything during a Honorlock quiz, when it locks the browser. The rest of the time it sits idle.

One proctor at a time

If you have Proctorio, ProctorU, or Examity extensions from another class, disable or remove them first. They block Honorlock from running.

On quiz day

What happens when you start.

The extension is already installed, so this part is quick. There is no camera, microphone, ID, or room scan. It is only a browser lockdown.

  1. Close extras and disconnect devices

    Before you start, close other apps and tabs and disconnect any second monitor or extra device. The lockdown will not begin if it detects more than one screen or another device.

  2. Open the quiz in Canvas

    Use the same Chrome where you installed the extension. Start within the window your instructor set, then click Launch Proctoring or Start.

  3. The browser locks down

    Once you start, you cannot open other tabs, windows, or apps until you submit. That is the whole point, and that is all Honorlock does here.

  4. Finish and submit

    When you submit your quiz, the lockdown lifts and your browser returns to normal.

If something goes wrong

Most problems have a one-minute fix.

Try these before quiz day so a glitch is never a surprise during the timed window.

Extension will not install or seems out of date

Open chrome://extensions, find Honorlock, and click Remove. Then relaunch the quiz in Canvas, which prompts a fresh install. Reinstalling before each quiz prevents most issues.

Another extension is interfering

In chrome://extensions, turn off everything else, especially ad blockers, Grammarly, and other proctoring tools. Re-enable them after your quiz if you like.

Shared computer or privacy concern

Create a separate Chrome profile just for exams, or simply remove the extension when you are done: right-click the Honorlock icon and choose Remove from Chrome.

Still stuck

Honorlock support runs 24/7. Use the live chat inside the exam window, call 844-243-2500, or email support@honorlock.com. If it is a course question, post in the Virtual Office Forum.
