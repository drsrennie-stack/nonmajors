How it works

Ask in the open. Everyone benefits.

Most questions a student has, three other students have too. Asking in the forum instead of a private email means the answer helps all of you, and you can read what has already been answered before you even post.

What to ask

Anything course-related

Content you are stuck on, a confusing lab workbook step, trouble with an assignment, a deadline question, a tech snag. If it touches the course, it belongs here.

Who answers

Me, or a classmate

If a classmate knows the answer and gets there before I do, they are welcome to answer. Helping someone else is one of the best ways to lock in your own understanding.

Response time

When you will hear back.

I read the forum regularly and answer in order. Plan ahead near a deadline, because a question posted at 10 PM the night something is due may not be answered in time.

Posted on a weekday
24 hours

Monday through Friday, expect a reply within one day.

Posted on a weekend
up to 72 hours

Friday evening through Sunday, allow up to three days. A classmate may answer sooner.

Before you post

Five rules that keep the forum useful.

These are not busywork. They are what makes the forum fast to search and fast to answer, for you and for everyone after you.

Search the forum first

Look through the existing questions before you post. If yours is already there, read that thread instead of starting a new one. Duplicates bury the answers everyone needs.

One question per post

Ask a single question at a time. If you have three things to ask, make three posts. One question per thread keeps each answer easy to find later.

Lead with the question

Put the actual question on the very first line. Then add the context underneath: which week and which assignment. The question goes on top, the explanation follows.

Show what you have already tried

List the steps you took to solve it on your own, what you found out when you tried, and the exact point where you got stuck. Name where the lack of clarity is, not just that you are confused. Pinpointing the confusion is what lets someone help you in one reply instead of five.

Answer if you can

If you scroll past a question you know the answer to, jump in. You do not have to wait for me. A class that helps itself learns faster.

The format

What a post that gets answered fast looks like.

Question first, in one clear line. Then your work: what you tried, what you found, and the exact spot you are stuck. Here is the shape to copy.

Week 4 · One question
Why does the action potential only travel in one direction down the axon?
What I tried
Reread the Week 4 pre-work on action potentials and watched the linked video twice. I drew the membrane and labeled the sodium and potassium channels.
What I found out
I can see that sodium channels open and depolarize the membrane, and that the impulse moves down the axon. So I understand how it moves forward.
Where I am stuck
I cannot picture what stops it from also going backward into the part that just fired. I think it is the refractory period, but I do not understand why that period only blocks the backward direction and not the forward one. That is the exact piece that is unclear to me.
