What you may have

A quiet room with the door closed, a single screen with other apps and devices disconnected, a steady internet connection, and water if you need it. That is all.

Important · read before you start

I do not have the Honorlock password. Honorlock does.

This is your practice run, so if Honorlock gives you any trouble, now is the time to fix it, not on a graded quiz. The rest of this still applies every time.

If Honorlock will not approve you or let you start, it is almost always because the requirements for the lockdown are not met. The most common causes are more than one browser open, extra tabs, a second monitor, or another device connected. The lockdown browser will not start until your setup is clean, so it will not let you begin.

When that happens, do not email me for a password. There is none for me to give you. Picture it: 11:50 at night, ten minutes left on the clock. I will not be awake to rescue you, and even if you did reach me, I still would not have a password to send.

If you hit a wall, start a live chat with Honorlock right away. They are available 24 hours a day and they are the only ones who can get you in. If chat is down, call 844-243-2500 or email support@honorlock.com.

So do not wait until the last minute. Open your quiz early in the window. That way, if something goes sideways, you still have time to chat with Honorlock and finish before it closes.

Before you click start

Quick readiness check.

Run through this once before you launch. Two minutes here saves a panicked email later.

  • Quiet room, door closed, phone in another room.

  • Only Google Chrome open, every other app and tab closed.

  • Honorlock extension installed and any other proctoring extensions disabled.

  • One screen only, second monitors and extra devices disconnected.

  • Strong internet in the spot where you are testing.

  • Plenty of time left in the window, not the last ten minutes.
