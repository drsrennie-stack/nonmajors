BIO 304 · Getting started

Tech setup. Nothing fancy.

This is a fully online course. Anything you already use for school will probably work. Here's what you actually need, what helps, and where to go when something breaks.

What you need

Required to take the course.

Eight items. Skip any of these and the course breaks down somewhere.

  • Required

    A computer or laptop

    Any Mac, Windows, or Chromebook from the last 5 or so years. Phones and tablets are not sufficient for proctored quizzes. Desktop or laptop required.

  • Required

    Reliable internet

    Enough to stream video comfortably. If you watch YouTube or Netflix without problems, you're fine for this course.

  • Required

    A modern browser

    Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge in their current versions. Canvas works best in Chrome and Firefox. Chrome is required for weekly quizzes (see Honorlock below). Keep your browser up to date.

  • Required

    Honorlock (Chrome extension)

    A free Chrome extension used only for weekly quizzes and lab exams. At ARC it's configured in lockdown-only mode: no webcam, no audio, no recording. It just prevents other tabs from being open during a quiz. Install instructions are in Module 0 before Week 1.

  • Required

    PDF reader and PDF creation

    Built into most operating systems. You'll submit weekly workbooks and portfolio pages as PDFs. The submission directions page walks through how to create PDFs from any platform (phone scan, Google Docs export, Word save-as).

  • Required

    Your Los Rios login

    You should already have this from registration. Use it to access Canvas at lrccd.instructure.com.

  • Required

    A printer

    Weekly workbooks and portfolio pages are designed to be printed and hand-labeled. Public libraries and campus printers count. If you cannot print, see the pen-and-paper note below for the alternative path.

  • Required

    A camera or scanning app

    Any phone camera works. Use a free scanning app (Adobe Scan, Microsoft Lens, Apple Notes, Google Drive) to capture handwritten work and convert it to a clean PDF for upload.

What helps

Not required, but they make this easier.

Three extras. None are gatekept by the course, but students who have them tend to stay ahead.

  • Recommended

    Blank paper and pens (if you skip printing)

    If printing isn't an option, hand-copy each workbook page to blank paper and complete it there. Same answers, same labels, same drawings. Scan and upload the result. This is the alternative path the workbooks are designed to support.

  • Recommended

    Colored pens or pencils

    Three to five colors. Helps a lot with anatomy drawings and labeling. Makes the work easier and the memorization stickier.

  • Recommended

    The Canvas app

    Canvas has free apps for iOS and Android. Useful for reading announcements on the go. Students who use it rarely miss deadlines.

If something isn't working

Where to go first.

Tech issues are real, and they usually aren't your fault. Two paths, depending on what broke.

Step 1, always

Canvas support, 24/7

For anything Canvas-related (login issues, assignments not loading, videos not playing, submissions failing), Canvas support is the fastest fix.

How to reach them. Click the Help icon (question mark) in the Canvas left sidebar. Live chat, phone, and email all work.

What to include. Your course name (BIO 304), what you were trying to do, and a screenshot of any error message.

Step 2, if Canvas is fine

Email me for course problems

If the course content itself has a problem (a broken link inside a page I built, a video with no audio, a lab missing a file), email me. I can fix those.

Email. rennies@arc.losrios.edu

Response time. Within 24 to 48 hours on weekdays. Weekend or holiday messages get a response on the next business day.
