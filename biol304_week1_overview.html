Welcome to Week 1

This week we lay the foundation. Levels of organization (atoms to organisms), what cells actually do, the four tissue types your body is built from, and the principle every body system has to obey: homeostasis. The vocabulary you build this week will show up every week after.

Take it slow on Day 1. The terminology will feel like a wall. By Wednesday, you will already start hearing the same words in the videos and the trainer and the readings, and the wall starts to feel like a doorway.

01

What You’ll Learn

Five concepts anchor this week. Each one connects to a video, a trainer section, and an OpenStax reading.

Concept 01

Levels of organization

From atoms to molecules to cells to tissues to organs to systems to the whole organism. The vocabulary that lets us talk about the body at any scale.

Concept 02

Anatomical terminology

Body planes (sagittal, frontal, transverse), directional terms (superior, distal, medial), body cavities, and regional names. Your shared language with every clinical chart.

Concept 03

Cell structure & function

The cell membrane, the major organelles, and how things move across membranes. Why every cell is doing the same basic jobs in different ways.

Concept 04

The four tissue types

Epithelial, connective, muscle, and nervous. Every organ in the body is built from some combination of these four. Recognize them and you have a key to the rest of the course.

Concept 05

Homeostasis

The body’s thermostat principle. How systems detect change and pull the body back to a working set point. The single idea this whole course turns on.

Concept 06

Biomolecules in brief

Carbohydrates, lipids, proteins, and nucleic acids. The four families of molecules every later week will keep referring back to.
