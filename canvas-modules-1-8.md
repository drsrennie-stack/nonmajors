**Question pool** (10 short-answer / multi-choice prompts pulled from this week's DOK 1 cards):

1. **[Levels of Organization]** List the levels of structural organization from smallest to largest.
   *Answer:* Chemical, cellular, tissue, organ, organ system, organism.

2. **[Levels of Organization]** How many organ systems make up the human body?
   *Answer:* Eleven.

3. **[Anatomical Terminology and Body Regions]** What plane divides the body into right and left equal halves?
   *Answer:* The midsagittal (median) plane.

4. **[Anatomical Terminology and Body Regions]** Is the knee proximal or distal to the ankle?
   *Answer:* Proximal.

5. **[Homeostasis and Feedback Loops]** Define homeostasis.
   *Answer:* The maintenance of a relatively stable internal environment despite external change.

6. **[Homeostasis and Feedback Loops]** List the four core components of a negative feedback loop.
   *Answer:* Stimulus, receptor, control center, effector (with a response that opposes the change).

7. **[Cell Structure and Organelles]** Where does cellular respiration occur?
   *Answer:* In the mitochondria.

8. **[Cell Structure and Organelles]** What is the function of the Golgi apparatus?
   *Answer:* Modifies, sorts, and packages proteins received from the rough ER.

9. **[Membrane Transport]** Define diffusion.
   *Answer:* Net movement of solute from a region of higher concentration to one of lower concentration.

10. **[Membrane Transport]** Which transport type uses ATP directly?
   *Answer:* Primary active transport.

3. **[Connective Tissues]** What three things make up the extracellular matrix?

4. **[Connective Tissues]** Which connective tissue type makes up tendons?

5. **[Muscle and Nervous Tissue Overview]** Which muscle tissue is striated and voluntary?

8. **[Skin Structure and Layers]** Which cell makes melanin?
   *Answer:* The melanocyte (in the stratum basale).

9. **[Skin Functions and Accessory Structures]** Name the sweat gland type responsible for thermoregulation.
   *Answer:* Eccrine.

10. **[Skin Functions and Accessory Structures]** What does sebum do?
   *Answer:* Lubricates hair and skin, waterproofs, and inhibits microbial growth.
