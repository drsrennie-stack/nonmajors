03

The workbooks

Two halves: anatomy then physiology

Lab is 50% anatomy and 50% physiology. Each week, those halves come as two separate workbooks. Anatomy first, then physiology. Both are required.

Each week: a paired set of workbooks

Half 1

Anatomy Workbook

Draw key structures using a reference figure (cell membrane, sarcomere, brain, nephron, etc.). Place the numbered structure list on your drawing. Answer synthesis questions. Word banks scaffold Weeks 1 and 2; from Week 3 onward, recall from your trainer practice.

Half 2

Physiology Workbook

Clinical dry lab simulation: tonicity calculations, DEXA interpretation, ECG reading, neuro exam, ABG analysis, and more. Patient cases. Final synthesis question integrates the entire week.

Why this design

Drawings & synthesis are graded heavily

Drawing tasks and synthesis questions cannot be copied from AI. They require genuine understanding and integration. Filling in label blanks is required, but the drawing and synthesis sections drive your grade.

Studies have shown: the act of drawing structures, even with a reference, builds spatial understanding far beyond what passive label-matching can achieve.

Why this design

Physiology is clinical

Each weekly physiology workbook teaches a real bedside skill: ECG interpretation, full neurological exam, ABG analysis, lab value interpretation. These prepare you for nursing school, EMT, paramedic, mortuary science, and any future A&P coursework you take.

Examples: Tonicity → burn fluids · Bone density → fracture risk · OGTT → diabetes diagnosis

04

The semester-long project

Clinical Reference Portfolio

A semester-long project where you build your own clinical reference document. One single-page reference per week, eight pages total, each aligned with that week's body system and the clinical tools we work through in the physiology workbook. By Week 8, you will have a complete clinical reference you can carry into nursing school, EMT, paramedic, mortuary science, or wherever your career takes you. Worth 15% of your final grade.

Deliverable 1

Weekly Portfolio Pages

8 single-sided reference pages, one per body system. Quick reference values, decision logic, two clinical scenarios, and an anatomy connection.

8 × 10 pts = 80 pts · Weeks 1 through 8

Deliverable 2

Final Compiled Portfolio

All 8 pages compiled into a single PDF with cover page and 200-400 word reflection. Submitted Friday of Week 8.

20 pts · Due Friday Week 8

05

How you are graded

Grade distribution

Five categories make up your final grade. Workbooks (Anatomy + Physiology combined) are the largest category. Lab exams come second. No late work is accepted.

Workbooks (Anatomy + Physiology) 30% 16 workbooks · 50 pts each
Lab Exams (Honorlock-proctored) 25% 2 exams · midterm Wk 4, final Wk 8
Clinical Reference Portfolio 15% 8 pages + final compilation
Weekly Quizzes (Honorlock) 15% 8 quizzes · 20 pts each
Discussions 15% 8 weeks · 25 pts each
Total 100% A 90+ · B 80+ · C 70+
