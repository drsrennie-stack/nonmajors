# BIO 304 Discussion Rubrics

The same grading structure applies to the Welcome Discussion (Module 0, 5 points) and every weekly discussion (Modules 1 to 8, 10 points each). One rubric, two point scales.

## The grading philosophy

Discussions are conversations. They only work if everyone shows up on time and contributes substantively. To enforce that without making the policy feel cruel, the rubric uses **hard ceilings** based on participation pattern:

| What the student did | What they can earn (ceiling) |
|---|---|
| On-time initial post + two substantive replies | Up to **100%** |
| On-time initial post + only one substantive reply (or two brief replies) | Up to **80%** |
| On-time initial post, no replies | Up to **60%** |
| Late initial post (after Friday 11:59 PM), with or without replies | Up to **40%** |
| No initial post and no replies | **0%** |

The "late initial = 40% max" rule exists because if the initial post arrives Saturday or Sunday, classmates have no time to read it and respond. The discussion fails for them. The penalty reflects the cost their lateness imposed on the class, not just on themselves.

This is the **only** exception to the course-wide "late work = 0" policy. Late discussion replies (after Sunday 11:59 PM) still earn 0 just like any other late submission.

## Single-criterion rubric (recommended)

Use one rubric criterion called **"Discussion participation"** with five rating levels. The levels embed the ceilings, so the score lands in the right band automatically. Bonus: graders see all five tiers in one column.

### For weekly discussions (10 points)

**Rubric Name:** Weekly Discussion
**Type:** Scale · **Rating Display:** Level · **Rating Order:** High &lt; Low · **Scoring:** Scored · **Total: 10 points**

**Criterion: Discussion participation**

| Rating level (left lowest → right highest) | Points | Description |
|---|---|---|
| No participation | 0 | No initial post and no replies. |
| Late initial | 4 | Initial post was not in by Friday 11:59 PM, with or without replies. Ceiling at 40%. |
| Initial only | 6 | On-time initial post but fewer than two substantive replies. Ceiling at 60%. |
| Mostly there | 8 | On-time initial post + replies posted but one reply is brief or one-line. |
| Full participation | 10 | On-time, substantive initial post (touches the prompt completely) + two substantive replies that build a connection or extend a classmate's thinking. |

### For the Welcome Discussion (3 points)

Tighter point spread, so collapse to four levels (the "mostly there" tier folds into "full" at this resolution):

**Rubric Name:** Welcome Discussion
**Type:** Scale · **Rating Display:** Level · **Rating Order:** High &lt; Low · **Scoring:** Scored · **Total: 3 points**

**Criterion: Discussion participation**

| Rating level (left lowest → right highest) | Points | Description |
|---|---|---|
| No participation | 0 | No initial post and no replies. |
| Late initial | 1 | Initial post not in by Friday 11:59 PM, with or without replies. Ceiling at roughly 33% (Canvas rounds the 40% cap down to 1 of 3). |
| Initial only | 2 | On-time initial post but fewer than two substantive replies. Ceiling at roughly 67% (Canvas rounds the 60% cap up to 2 of 3). |
| Full participation | 3 | On-time, substantive initial post (touches all five prompt items) + two substantive replies that build a connection with a classmate. |

## Two-criterion rubric (alternative, if you want timing and replies graded separately)

Use this if you want the gradebook to show timing and reply quality as separate columns. The math still produces the same ceilings if you grade strictly. **Most graders find the single-criterion version simpler.**

### For weekly discussions (10 points)

**Criterion 1: On-time initial post (6 points)**

| Rating level | Points | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Missing | 0 | No initial post at all. |
| Late (after Fri 11:59 PM) | 2 | Initial post arrived after Friday's deadline. (Capped at 2 of 6 to enforce 40% ceiling on the whole score.) |
| On time, partial | 4 | On-time post but does not address the full prompt. |
| On time, substantive | 6 | On-time post that addresses the full prompt with evidence. |

**Criterion 2: Substantive replies (4 points)**

| Rating level | Points | Description |
|---|---|---|
| None | 0 | Zero or one reply. |
| Partial | 2 | Two replies but one is brief or one-line. |
| Both substantive | 4 | Two thoughtful replies to two different classmates. |

If you use this split, write the late-ceiling rule into your reading of Criterion 1 so a late initial post can never exceed 2 points (40% of 6). The single-criterion rubric is cleaner.

### For the Welcome Discussion (5 points)

Same split, scaled:

**Criterion 1: On-time initial post (3 points)**: 0 / 1 (late) / 2 (on time, partial) / 3 (on time, complete)
**Criterion 2: Substantive replies (2 points)**: 0 / 1 (partial) / 2 (both substantive)

## How to enter the recommended rubric in Canvas

1. Open the discussion or assignment, scroll to **+ Rubric**.
2. Click **Create New Rubric**.
3. Fill **Rubric Name**, set **Type: Scale**, **Rating Display: Level**, **Rating Order: High &lt; Low**, **Scoring: Scored**.
4. Check **Use this rubric for assignment grading**.
5. Click **Draft New Criterion**. Set the name to **Discussion participation**.
6. Add five rating columns (Canvas may default to four; add a fifth with the **+** button).
7. Paste the five rating descriptions and point values from the table above.
8. Confirm the top right shows **10 Points Possible** (or **5** for the welcome).
9. Save and attach.
10. For the seven remaining weekly discussions: open the rubric from your account-level rubric library and reuse, OR duplicate the same structure each week.
