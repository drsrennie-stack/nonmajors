# What happened with your engagement score

Short version: you did nothing wrong, and you were not losing credit for skipping work. There was a bug in how one part of the score was calculated, and it has been fixed.

## Why it dropped from 81% to 64%

Your engagement score is built from three parts:

- Coverage (50%): how many cards you have seen at least once
- Consistency (30%): doing your reviews on study days
- Depth (20%): how far up the difficulty ladder you have worked

Your Coverage was 100% and your Depth was solid. The drop came entirely from Consistency.

The Consistency part is supposed to reward clearing your due reviews each study day. The problem was in the timing of when the tool started recording a "cleared day." That recording only began very recently. So the moment you finished a daily review and the tool logged its very first cleared day, it switched into a stricter counting mode and only counted that one day, ignoring all the days you had already studied. That is why finishing your review made the number go down instead of up. It was the tool, not you.

## What the fix changes

- Every study day you have already completed is now credited. Your past work counts.
- From here on, a day counts toward Consistency when you clear your due reviews that day.
- Finishing your reviews will never lower your score again. It can only hold steady or go up.

Your existing progress, card history, and streak were not touched or reset.

## What to do each day

Plan on about 30 minutes a day: open the tool, clear the cards that are due, and that day is credited. You do not need to do more than that. If you want to push ahead or review extra, you are welcome to, and it will not hurt your score.

If your number ever looks off again, export your backup from the report page and send it over so it can be checked against the source.
