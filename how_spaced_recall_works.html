How spaced recall works

A short read. Saves you hours later.

Why this works

Within 24 hours of learning something new, most of it is gone unless you actively pull it back up. That drop is called the forgetting curve. Spaced recall flattens it. The system shows you each card right before you would have forgotten it, you try to remember the answer, and you rate how it felt. Cards you struggle with come back tomorrow. Cards you nailed come back in three days, then a week, then two. The system tracks all of this for you. You just show up and rate honestly.

The forgetting curve

Each navy dot is a review session. You drill the card, the curve resets, the next gap gets longer. That's why daily review beats Saturday cramming.

After each card, you rate it

How you rate is how the system schedules. Be honest. The numbers are approximate, the system adjusts based on your overall performance.
