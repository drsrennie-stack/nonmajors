Why this works

Forgetting is normal. Spaced recall is the fix.

Your brain forgets most of what it sees the first time. Hermann Ebbinghaus mapped this in the 1880s and called it the forgetting curve: about 24 hours after learning something new, more than half of it is gone. Spaced recall flips that curve by bringing each fact back exactly when you are about to forget it. Every time you successfully pull something out of memory, the next reminder pushes farther into the future, and the memory gets sturdier. You do not have to plan any of this. The pre-work hub tracks every card you have seen, every rating you have given it, and surfaces the cards that are due each day. Your job is to show up.

Dashed terra line: what you remember if you study once and never come back. Solid line with spikes: what you remember when the system surfaces a card and you successfully recall it. Each spike resets the curve a little higher and a little flatter.

How the system tracks you

You do not have to remember which cards are due. The system does.

Every card you see gets a rating from you: Again, Hard, Good, or Easy. The system uses your rating to decide when that card comes back. Struggle cards return tomorrow. Solid cards return in a few days. Cards you mark Easy push out to a week or longer. When you click Daily review in the top nav, the system pulls every card whose due date is today or in the past, sorts the toughest ones to the top, and lets you work through them in one stream.

This is why the rule is "show up every day." If you skip a day, all of yesterday's cards stack onto today and you spend twice as long working through them. Daily engagement is the cheapest way to keep your time low.
