Course Map

Eight weeks · Sixteen modules · 44 one-pager sheets

Every sheet has three modes. Notes prints the full reference. Study prints as a fill-in-the-blank worksheet to complete during the video or reading. Quiz me is on-screen typing practice for vocabulary. Each sheet also links out to its spaced-recall pre-work once the video is watched.

Click a topic to open its one-pager · 44 total

Week 1

Module 1 · Foundations of Anatomy & Physiology

Levels of Organization Atoms to organism — six levels of complexity and what emerges at each. Anatomical Position, Planes & Directional Terms The standard body map and the vocabulary for talking about it. Body Regions Regional terms across anterior, posterior, and lateral views. Body Cavities & Regions Dorsal/ventral cavities, abdominopelvic regions and quadrants. Homeostasis & Feedback Loops Negative feedback as the default; positive feedback as the exception.

Module 2 · The Cell

Cell Structure & Organelles Membrane, nucleus, and the cytoplasmic organelles that do the work. Membrane Transport Passive, active, and bulk transport across the plasma membrane.

Week 2

Module 3 · Tissues

Epithelial Tissue Classification Classification by shape and layers; exocrine vs endocrine. Connective Tissues ECM components and the loose, dense, and specialized CTs. Muscle & Nervous Tissue Overview Three muscle types and the cells of the nervous system.

Module 4 · Integumentary System

Skin Structure & Layers Epidermal strata, dermal layers, and the hypodermis. Skin Functions & Accessory Structures Six functions plus hair, nails, and the cutaneous glands.

Week 3

Module 5 · Skeletal System

Bone Tissue & Bone Growth Bone cells, compact and spongy structure, ossification, calcium balance. Axial Skeleton Skull, vertebral column, and thoracic cage. Appendicular Skeleton Pectoral and pelvic girdles plus the limbs. Joints & Body Movements Joint classifications and the catalog of movements.

Week 4

Module 6 · Muscular System

Skeletal Muscle Microanatomy From whole muscle down to the sarcomere. Motor Units & Muscle Mechanics Recruitment, summation, twitch and tetanus, fiber types. Sliding Filament & Cross-Bridge Cycle Excitation-contraction coupling and the four-step cycle.

Module 7 · Nervous System

Neurons & Resting Membrane Potential Neuron types and how the membrane sits at -70 mV. Action Potentials & Synaptic Transmission AP phases, propagation, and the chemical synapse. CNS Organization: Brain & Spinal Cord Brain regions, lobes, spinal cord, meninges, CSF flow. PNS & Autonomic Nervous System Cranial nerves, somatic vs autonomic, sympathetic vs parasympathetic.

Week 5

Module 8 · Special Senses

Vision Eye anatomy, retinal layers, and the visual pathway. Hearing & Equilibrium Outer, middle, and inner ear; cochlea and vestibular apparatus.

Module 9 · Endocrine System

Hormone Mechanisms Peptide vs steroid; second messengers and gene activation. Major Endocrine Glands Hypothalamus to gonads, with the hormones each makes.

Week 6

Module 10 · Blood

Blood Composition & Hemopoiesis Plasma, formed elements, and the hemopoiesis tree. Hemostasis & Blood Typing Three phases of clotting, ABO and Rh compatibility.

Module 11 · Cardiovascular System

Heart Anatomy & the Cardiac Cycle Four chambers, four valves, systole and diastole. Cardiac Conduction System SA to Purkinje, two AP types, and reading an ECG. Blood Vessels & Hemodynamics Vessel structure, pressure profile, and Starling forces.

Week 7

Module 12 · Lymphatic & Immune System

Lymphatic System & Innate Immunity Lymphatic plumbing and first-line defenses. Adaptive Immunity T cells, B cells, antibodies, and immunologic memory.

Module 13 · Respiratory System

Respiratory Anatomy & Mechanics Conducting vs respiratory zone; breathing as pressure work. Gas Exchange & Transport Partial pressures, oxyhemoglobin curve, and CO₂ transport.

Module 14 · Digestive System

GI Tract Anatomy & Motility From mouth to anus; four wall layers; peristalsis and segmentation. Digestion & Absorption Macromolecules broken down and absorbed at the brush border.

Week 8

Module 15 · Urinary System & Fluid Balance

Kidney Anatomy & Glomerular Filtration Nephron parts, the filtration barrier, and how GFR is regulated. Tubular Function & Urine Concentration Segment-by-segment reabsorption, countercurrent multiplication. Fluid, Electrolyte & Acid-Base Balance Body fluid compartments, key electrolytes, ABG analysis.

Module 16 · Reproductive System

Male Reproductive System Sperm path, spermatogenesis, HPG axis. Female Reproductive System Ovaries, oogenesis, and the 28-day cycle. Pregnancy A&P (Basics) Fertilization, placenta, maternal adaptations, stages of labor.
