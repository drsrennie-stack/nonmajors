How each week works

A steady rhythm, Monday through Thursday.

Pre-work nightly, class four days a week. Once the pattern clicks, the workload feels predictable.

  1. 01
    Nightly

    Pre-work

    Lecture videos (about 90 minutes), active recall cards, and workbook pages. Plan on at least 3 focused hours per night.

  2. 02
    Monday

    Lab exam

    After Week 1, every Monday opens with a fill-in-the-blank lab exam on the previous week.

  3. 03
    Tuesday

    Active learning & success coaching

    Identifying structures and reasoning through the week's material together, with study and recovery strategies woven in.

  4. 04
    Wednesday

    SIM case

    A clinical simulation case that puts the week's anatomy into a real scenario you reason through as a team.

  5. 05
    Thursday

    Team-based learning

    The week's biggest application problem, solved with your team. iRAT, tRAT, and a 4S application activity.

Get unstuck

Help is built into how this course runs.

Start with your team and the weekly module. If you're still stuck, the answers and the door are right here.

What should I do before the first day? +

Five things to walk in ready:

  1. Read the syllabus end to end.
  2. Sign the device policy.
  3. Learn where to find your textbook, the cadaver image library, and the rest of your course resources.
  4. Read the overview of how Team-Based Learning runs in this course.
  5. Take the syllabus quiz.
How do exams work in this course? +

Your grade has four parts. Lab exams are 45%: they run Mondays, each covering the previous week, with fill-in-the-blank identification on histology, slides, models, and cadaver. The weekly TBL is another 45%. A cumulative lecture final at term's end is 5%, and short end-of-day lab quizzes are the last 5%. Dates live in your syllabus.

What if I miss a class? +

This is a strict attendance policy: students are dropped from the course after three absences, no exceptions. The eight-week format moves quickly and the material does not pause for catch-up. If something comes up, message me as early as possible.

Do I need to buy anything? +

Three things to plan for:

  1. InteDashboard access, about $40. We will set it up together on the first day of class.
  2. A lab coat that stays in the cadaver lab.
  3. Gloves. You can split a box with your teammates to save on cost.

Bring something to draw with as well. You will sketch and label structures by hand throughout the course. The full required and recommended list is in the syllabus.

Where do I get help when I'm stuck? +

Start with your team and the weekly module. For anything beyond that, office hours and contact details are in the card to the right. You are never stuck alone in this course.

Why so much drawing? +

Anatomy you can name from a finished textbook isn't the same as anatomy you can identify on a body. Drawing forces you to put each structure in space yourself, which is how it actually moves into memory. Neat art is not the goal. Understanding is.

Meet your instructor

A decade of teaching anatomy, in your corner.

I'm Dr. Sharilyn Rennie. I've taught anatomy, physiology, and the medical sciences for over a decade across California community colleges, and before teaching I spent years in clinical medicine. That clinical background shapes how I teach: every structure connects to something real that happens in a body.

Anatomy is a language. My job this summer is to help you become fluent in it, one week at a time, with a clear path and a team beside you. Come curious, come prepared, and ask questions often.
