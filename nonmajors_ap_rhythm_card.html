01

The Term

Eight weeks. One body system per week. Each one pairs anatomy and physiology with a clinical tool you’ll use in your future program.

1Week

Body Basics · Cells & Tissues

Levels of organization, cell function, the four tissue types, and homeostasis as the rule every system follows.

2Week

Protection & Support

Skin as first barrier, bone and joints, and skeletal muscle. The structures that protect, support, and move the body.

3Week

Control Systems

Nervous and endocrine basics. Fast electrical signaling, slower chemical signaling, and how the two coordinate.

4Week

Senses & Integration

The five senses, major brain regions, reflex arcs, and how the brain turns signals into perception and action.

5Week

Transport

Heart and vessels, blood composition, circulation, and the immune basics that keep the system intact.

6Week

Gas Exchange & Waste

Breathing mechanics, gas exchange at the alveolus, kidney filtration, and how the body balances water and waste.

7Week

Nutrition

Digestive anatomy, mechanical and chemical breakdown, absorption, basic metabolism, and the liver as metabolic hub.

8Week

Life Stages · Final Synthesis

Reproductive basics, pregnancy and development, aging physiology, and how the systems work together across a life.

03

Four Tracks, One Week

Every week pulls from the same four tracks. You’ll know the rhythm by Week 2.

Track 01

Lecture

About 90 minutes of concept videos per week, broken into 4 to 6 chunks of 15 to 20 minutes. Watch at your own pace. A short weekly quiz checks the concepts, taken with Honorlock in lockdown-only mode (two attempts, best score kept, due Sunday). Friday closes with a shorter synthesis Loom.

Track 02

Discussion

Weekly async forum. The prompt opens Wednesday, after you’ve worked through most of the concept videos. Your first post is due Friday so classmates have the weekend to read and reply. Replies to classmates are due Sunday.

Track 03

Trainer

One trainer per week, built around the 4 to 6 concept videos. Print it at the start of the week. Fill in each section as you watch the matching video. Photograph and upload by Sunday.

Track 04

Lab

Two parts, one submission each week, due Sunday. In the anatomy half you label structures on OpenStax figures. In the physiology half you use a clinical risk calculator and interpret what the numbers mean.

Why the week is built this way

Summer online courses tend to burn people out. This one is shaped against that. Most of your work lands Sunday end of day. You can aim to finish by Friday if you want your weekend free, or use the weekend if you need to. Same credit either way, same grade ceiling.

There is one earlier deadline: your first discussion post is due Friday so classmates have the weekend to read it and reply. Replies themselves are due Sunday with everything else. No penalty for finishing early.

Late policy

Everything is due Sunday end of day, with one exception: your first discussion post is due Friday so classmates have time to reply. Submissions within 24 hours of either deadline earn 50% of the points they would have earned on time. After 24 hours, the grade is zero. No exceptions.
