01 How to use this template

Before you start

This template is a starting structure. The four sections below match the rubric exactly. Your job is to fill in the values, the decision logic, and the scenario responses, then print the final document to one side of one sheet.

One page only

Your final submission must fit on one printable page. The template includes response spaces that take more room than your final answer will. Compress as you finalize. If you cannot fit it, cut, do not extend.

You may keep the template's structure or rebuild it in your own format, as long as your finished page contains all four required sections (Quick Reference, Decision Logic, Patient Scenarios, Integration sentence).

04 Patient Scenarios

Where the page meets a real situation

For each scenario, write your reasoning in the response space. Show the steps; do not just give the answer.

Scenario 1
A 45-year-old, three days post-anoxic injury, no spontaneous respiration on apnea test (PaCO2 rises from 40 to 65), pupils fixed, no brainstem reflexes.
Walk through the criteria you have met and the next steps before declaration.
Scenario 2
A 6-month-old and an 80-year-old both present with HR 150 and the same complaint of fever and lethargy.
How do you weight the two findings differently? What's concerning about each?
Scenario 3
A 78-year-old on lisinopril, furosemide, atorvastatin, metformin, gabapentin, and aspirin, presents with a fall.
What category of complication is on your list first, and what is your screen?
05 Integration

Connecting this week to the rest

One sentence connecting this week to a prior system

Synthesize across all eight weeks: pick one organ system that has shown up in at least three of your prior portfolio pages and write one sentence about how its failure cascades into the others. This is your final integration.
