Structural hierarchy (big to small)

Whole muscle

  • Wrapped in epimysium (dense irregular CT).

Fascicle

  • Bundle of muscle fibers wrapped in perimysium.

Muscle fiber (cell)

  • Single multinucleate cell wrapped in endomysium.
  • Plasma membrane = sarcolemma. Cytoplasm = sarcoplasm.

Myofibril

  • Rod-shaped contractile organelle inside the fiber. Hundreds per fiber.

Sarcomere

  • Functional unit. Z-line to Z-line. Hundreds end-to-end down each myofibril.

The sarcomere

Z-line

  • Sarcomere boundary. Thin filaments anchor here.

I band

  • Thin filaments only (light under microscopy). Shortens during contraction.

A band

  • Full length of thick filament (dark band). Width never changes during contraction.

H zone

  • Thick filaments only, no overlap. Shortens during contraction.

M line

  • Center of sarcomere. Thick filaments anchor here.

Excitation machinery

T-tubules

  • Invaginations of sarcolemma. Carry the action potential deep into the fiber.

Sarcoplasmic reticulum

  • Smooth ER specialized for Ca2+ storage. Releases Ca2+ when the T-tubule signal arrives.

Clinical tie-in

Where the microanatomy shows up clinically

Duchenne muscular dystrophy is a defect in the protein dystrophin, which anchors the sarcomere's contractile machinery to the sarcolemma. Without it, every contraction tears the muscle fiber. Statin-induced myopathy disrupts the sarcoplasmic reticulum's calcium handling. The CK lab value rises when sarcolemma is breached. Microanatomy is what fails first, even when the patient just feels weak.
