01

Gross anatomy

Parts of a Long Bone

Concept

From outside in, top to bottom

  • Diaphysis shaft
  • Compact bone wall, medullary cavity with yellow marrow
  • Epiphysis ends
  • Spongy bone with red marrow, articular cartilage on joint surface
  • Metaphysis growth zone
  • Contains epiphyseal plate (growth plate) until ~18-21 yrs
  • Periosteum outer membrane (vessels, nerves, attachment)
  • Endosteum lines medullary cavity (remodeling)
Takeaway: compact bone is the shaft (resists compression). Spongy bone is the ends (distributes load to joints, holds red marrow). Yellow marrow stores fat; red marrow makes blood.
02

Cells

Four Bone Cells

Reference

Each cell, one job

  • Osteogenic stem
  • Only bone cell that divides. In periosteum + endosteum.
  • Osteoblast build
  • Secretes matrix + initiates calcification
  • Osteocyte maintain
  • Trapped osteoblast, in lacunae, communicates via canaliculi
  • Osteoclast resorb
  • Multinucleated, from monocytes (not osteogenic line)
Mnemonic: blast Builds, clast Crushes, cyte siCs in place. Genic = stem cell.

Compact bone

The Osteon

Draw

Structural unit of compact bone

  • Concentric lamellae around a central canal
  • Central (Haversian) canal with blood vessel + nerve
  • Perforating (Volkmann) canals connect canals at right angles
  • Lacunae between lamellae house osteocytes
  • Canaliculi connect lacunae for nutrient exchange
  • Interstitial lamellae fill gaps between osteons
  • Circumferential lamellae line outer + inner surfaces
Draw: a cross-section of an osteon. Concentric circles (lamellae) around a central canal with a vessel inside. Dot small lacunae between lamellae with thin canaliculi connecting them. Add a perforating canal cutting horizontally.
04

Spongy bone

Trabeculae & Why

Concept

The lattice that lightens the load

  • Trabeculae are matrix spikes arranged along stress lines
  • Osteocytes in lacunae, no concentric rings
  • Spaces hold red marrow (hematopoiesis)
  • Found at epiphyses of long bones
  • Found between layers of flat bones (diploe)
  • Lighter than compact, less strong but adaptable
  • Remodels in response to mechanical demand (Wolff's law)
Takeaway: compact bone is dense and stupid (uniform). Spongy bone is light and smart (orients to stress). Together they balance weight and strength.

Bone markings

Surface Features

Draw

Three categories

  • Articulations joints
  • Head, facet, condyle
  • Projections attachment
  • Process, tubercle, tuberosity, spine, crest, line
  • Holes passage
  • Foramen, fossa, fovea, sulcus, canal, fissure, meatus, sinus
  • Size + location reflect forces or vessels passing through
Draw: the proximal end of a femur. Label the head (articulation), greater + lesser trochanters (projections), neck, and intertrochanteric line. Add a fovea capitis on the head.
06

Clinical

Remodeling Gone Wrong

Draw

When osteoblasts and osteoclasts mismatch

  • Osteoporosis net resorption
  • Postmenopausal estrogen loss accelerates osteoclast activity
  • Osteopenia precursor
  • Paget's disease overactive both
  • Excess remodeling, weak brittle replacement bone
  • Rickets/osteomalacia low Vit D
  • Treatment: bisphosphonates slow osteoclasts, hormone therapy, weight-bearing exercise
Draw: two side-by-side trabecular bone sections. Left = healthy (thick interconnected spikes). Right = osteoporotic (thin, fewer connections, larger gaps). Same scale.
