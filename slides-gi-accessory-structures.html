01

Salivary glands

Three Pairs, Three Ducts

Draw

Saliva starts digestion

  • Parotid (largest): anterior to the ear, drains via Stensen duct opposite the upper 2nd molar; mostly serous
  • Submandibular: under the mandible body, drains via Wharton duct at the floor of the mouth; mixed serous + mucous
  • Sublingual: smallest, under the tongue, multiple short ducts open along the floor of mouth; mostly mucous
  • Saliva contents: water, mucin, bicarbonate, salivary amylase (carbs), lingual lipase (fats), lysozyme + IgA (defense)
  • Daily output: ~1 to 1.5 L
  • Autonomic control: parasympathetic increases watery saliva, sympathetic produces thicker mucous saliva (dry-mouth-when-nervous)
  • Clinical: mumps inflames the parotid, stones (sialolithiasis) most often form in the submandibular's longer upward-running duct
Draw: a left lateral face showing all three salivary glands with the correct ducts. Mark the Stensen duct opening near the upper 2nd molar and the Wharton duct opening at the sublingual caruncle.
02

Liver

Liver, Largest Internal Organ

Draw

Anatomy and dual blood supply

  • ~1.5 kg, right upper quadrant, under the diaphragm
  • Four anatomical lobes: right (largest), left, caudate (posterior), quadrate (inferior)
  • Falciform ligament: peritoneal fold separating right and left lobes anteriorly, holds the round ligament (ligamentum teres, remnant of fetal umbilical vein)
  • Porta hepatis: hilum where vessels and ducts enter and exit
  • Dual blood supply:
  • Hepatic artery proper (~25% of flow, ~50% of O2): oxygenated blood from celiac trunk
  • Hepatic portal vein (~75% of flow): nutrient-rich, deoxygenated blood draining the GI tract and spleen
  • Hepatic veins: drain into the IVC, no extrahepatic course
  • Functional unit: the hepatic lobule, hexagonal, with portal triads at the corners (artery + portal vein branch + bile duct) and a central vein
  • Functions: bile production, glucose buffering (glycogen storage), protein synthesis (albumin, clotting factors), detoxification (CYP enzymes), urea cycle, vitamin storage
Draw: the liver in situ from anterior view. Show all four lobes, the falciform ligament with round ligament, gallbladder tucked under the right lobe, and arrows for the three vessel inputs/outputs at the porta hepatis.
03

Gallbladder + biliary tree

Bile Storage and Delivery

Draw

From hepatocyte to duodenum

  • Bile is made continuously by hepatocytes (water, bile salts, cholesterol, bilirubin, electrolytes)
  • Right + left hepatic ducts → merge into the common hepatic duct
  • Cystic duct branches off to the gallbladder
  • Common hepatic + cystic = common bile duct (CBD)
  • CBD joins the main pancreatic duct at the ampulla of Vater
  • The ampulla opens into the duodenum through the major duodenal papilla, gated by the sphincter of Oddi
  • Gallbladder: small pear-shaped sac under the right liver lobe, stores + concentrates bile (~5 to 10x by water reabsorption)
  • CCK (released from duodenal I cells in response to fat) contracts the gallbladder and relaxes the sphincter of Oddi, ejecting bile into the duodenum
  • Clinical: gallstones (cholelithiasis), cholecystitis, jaundice when CBD is obstructed
Draw: the biliary tree from the liver down. Label both hepatic ducts, common hepatic, cystic, common bile duct, ampulla of Vater, sphincter of Oddi, and the duodenal opening at the major duodenal papilla.
04

Pancreas

Pancreas, Exocrine and Endocrine

Draw

Two organs in one gland

  • Location: retroperitoneal, sits posterior to the stomach, head in the C-loop of the duodenum, body and tail extending toward the spleen
  • Exocrine ~99% of mass: acinar cells secrete digestive enzymes (trypsinogen, chymotrypsinogen, lipase, amylase, nucleases) + duct cells secrete bicarbonate-rich fluid
  • Enzymes are stored as inactive zymogens to prevent autodigestion
  • Main pancreatic duct (duct of Wirsung): runs the length of the gland, joins the CBD at the ampulla of Vater
  • Accessory pancreatic duct (duct of Santorini): present in many people, opens at the minor duodenal papilla
  • Endocrine ~1 to 2%: islets of Langerhans, scattered through the gland (mostly tail)
  • Islet cells (covered in endocrine deck): alpha (glucagon), beta (insulin), delta (somatostatin), PP (pancreatic polypeptide)
  • Clinical: pancreatitis when enzymes activate inside the gland, pancreatic cancer in the head causes painless jaundice by squeezing the CBD
Draw: a posterior view of the pancreas + duodenum + CBD. Label head, body, tail, both pancreatic ducts, the C-loop of the duodenum, the ampulla of Vater, and one zoomed inset showing an acinus + a single islet.
05

Appendix

Appendix, Not Quite Vestigial

Concept

Small, useful, and famous for inflammation

  • Location: hangs off the cecum, posteromedial side, in the right lower quadrant
  • McBurney point: surface landmark, one-third of the way from the right ASIS to the umbilicus; classic site of maximal tenderness in appendicitis
  • Length: ~9 cm average, variable
  • Histology: same 4 wall layers, with abundant lymphoid follicles in the submucosa
  • Two emerging functions:
  • Immune surveillance: gut-associated lymphoid tissue (GALT), samples antigens passing the cecum
  • Microbiome reservoir: a safe house where commensal bacteria persist during diarrheal illness and reseed the colon afterward
  • Appendicitis: luminal obstruction (fecalith, lymphoid hyperplasia) → inflammation → risk of perforation if untreated
  • Classic presentation: periumbilical pain that migrates to RLQ + low-grade fever + leukocytosis
Takeaway: the appendix is not just a remnant. Calling it "useless" undersells its role in immunity and microbiome resilience, even though humans tolerate appendectomy fine. Inflammation is what makes it surgical, not its baseline function.

Dr. Sharilyn Rennie
