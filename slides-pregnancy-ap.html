Fertilization

Fertilization

Concept

Sperm meets egg

  • Occurs in fallopian tube ampulla, usually within 24 hours of ovulation
  • Capacitation: sperm membrane changes in female tract
  • Acrosomal reaction: sperm releases enzymes to penetrate zona pellucida
  • Cortical reaction: egg blocks polyspermy after one sperm enters
  • Diploid zygote forms; meiosis II completes in egg
  • Day 0: fertilization. Day 6-7: implantation begins
Takeaway: only the head + neck of sperm enter the egg. Tail is discarded. Egg activation triggers calcium waves that block other sperm and complete oocyte meiosis.

Cleavage

Cleavage + Implantation

Draw

Zygote to blastocyst

  • Day 1-3: cleavage divisions (2 → 4 → 8 cells)
  • Day 4: morula (~16 cells)
  • Day 5: blastocyst forms (inner cell mass + trophoblast + cavity)
  • Day 6-7: blastocyst hatches from zona, implants in endometrium
  • Trophoblast → placenta
  • Inner cell mass → embryo
  • hCG from trophoblast maintains corpus luteum
Draw: a timeline diagram. Day 0 (fertilization in tube) → Day 3 (morula traveling) → Day 5 (blastocyst arrives at uterus) → Day 6-7 (implantation begins). Show structure changes at each step.
03

Germ layers

Gastrulation & Germ Layers

Concept

Three layers from one

  • Around week 3, embryonic disc forms 3 germ layers
  • Ectoderm: nervous system, epidermis, hair, eye lens
  • Mesoderm: muscles, bones, heart, kidneys, blood, connective tissues
  • Endoderm: GI lining, lungs, liver, pancreas, bladder
  • All adult tissues trace back to one of these
  • Neurulation: ectoderm folds to form neural tube (becomes CNS)
  • Neural tube defects (spina bifida, anencephaly) preventable by folic acid
Takeaway: derm-from-derm: epi = skin (ecto), endo = inside (gut), meso = middle (everything in between). Knowing germ layer origins helps explain why some structures share embryologic kinship (e.g., kidney + gonad both mesoderm).
04

Placenta

Placenta Structure & Function

Draw

Maternal + fetal interface

  • Maternal side: decidua basalis (modified endometrium)
  • Fetal side: chorionic villi with fetal capillaries
  • Intervillous space bathes villi in maternal blood
  • No direct blood mixing, all exchange via diffusion across villi
  • Functions: gas exchange, nutrient + waste transfer, hormone production (hCG, hPL, estriol, progesterone), immune barrier
  • Umbilical cord: 2 arteries (deoxygenated fetal → placenta) + 1 vein (oxygenated → fetus)
Draw: a placenta cross-section. Maternal blood pools in intervillous space. Fetal villi project into it. Show arrows: maternal O₂/nutrients into fetus, fetal CO₂/waste back to maternal blood. Add umbilical cord with 2 arteries + 1 vein.
05

Maternal

Maternal Physiological Changes

Concept

Every system adapts

  • Cardiovascular: blood volume ↑ 30-50%, CO ↑ 30%, mild anemia (dilutional)
  • Respiratory: tidal volume ↑, mild respiratory alkalosis
  • Renal: GFR ↑ ~50%, glucosuria possible (threshold drops)
  • GI: motility slowed by progesterone; reflux + constipation common
  • Endocrine: hCG (early), hPL (lactogen, insulin resistance), relaxin (ligament laxity)
  • Hematologic: hypercoagulable state (DVT/PE risk)
  • Musculoskeletal: lordosis to balance load
Takeaway: pregnancy is an extreme physiological state. Many normal lab values change. Always interpret labs against pregnancy-specific reference ranges.
06

Clinical

Pregnancy Complications & Labor

Concept

When things go wrong

  • Trimesters: 1st (1-12 wk), 2nd (13-26), 3rd (27-40)
  • Ectopic pregnancy: implants outside uterus (often tube), life-threatening
  • Miscarriage: ~10-20% of recognized pregnancies
  • Preeclampsia: HTN + proteinuria after 20 weeks; eclampsia adds seizures
  • Gestational diabetes: insulin resistance overshoots
  • Placenta previa: placenta covers cervix
  • Labor: oxytocin + prostaglandin drive contractions (positive feedback)
  • 3 stages: dilation, expulsion, placental
Takeaway: oxytocin during labor is the textbook example of positive feedback. Stretch on cervix → oxytocin → contractions → more stretch → more oxytocin. Stops when baby + placenta are delivered.
