Functions, part one

Protection & Sensation

Concept

Barriers + receptors

  • Physical barrier stratum corneum
  • Keratin + lipids = water resistance
  • UV protection melanin
  • Immune barrier Langerhans
  • Sensation multiple receptors
  • Meissner (light touch, papillary)
  • Pacinian (deep pressure, reticular/hypodermis)
  • Merkel discs (sustained touch)
  • Ruffini (stretch), free nerve endings (pain, temp)
Takeaway: skin is a multi-modal sensor and a multi-modal barrier. Different receptors at different depths report different stimuli to the brain.
02

Hair

Hair Follicle Anatomy

Draw

From bulb to shaft

  • Hair bulb at base, in dermis
  • Papilla with capillaries inside bulb
  • Matrix of dividing cells
  • Root below skin surface
  • Shaft above skin surface
  • 3 concentric layers: medulla, cortex, cuticle
  • Arrector pili smooth muscle (goosebumps)
  • Sebaceous gland opens into follicle
Draw: a hair follicle in cross-section. Bulb at bottom with papilla and capillaries. Shaft emerging through skin. Add arrector pili muscle and a sebaceous gland feeding into the follicle.
03

Nails

Nail Anatomy

Concept

Parts of a nail

  • Nail body visible plate
  • Free edge distal
  • Nail root proximal, under skin
  • Nail matrix growth zone
  • Lunula visible matrix (the moon)
  • Eponychium (cuticle) proximal fold
  • Hyponychium under free edge
  • Made of dead keratinized cells (like stratum corneum)
Takeaway: nails are heavily keratinized epidermis. The matrix is the only living part. Nail bed color reflects capillary blood (pink = good perfusion, pale/blue = poor).
04

Glands

Sweat & Sebaceous Glands

Draw

Two sweats + one oil

  • Eccrine sweat most of body
  • Watery sweat, opens to pore on skin surface, thermoregulation
  • Apocrine sweat axilla, groin
  • Thicker secretion, opens into hair follicle, activated at puberty
  • Sebaceous holocrine
  • Secrete sebum (oil) into hair follicles
  • Ceruminous (ear wax) and mammary glands are modified versions
Draw: a section of dermis with three glands side-by-side. Eccrine (duct to skin pore), apocrine (duct to hair follicle), sebaceous (duct to follicle, holocrine releasing whole cells). Label and arrow each duct.
05

Functions, part two

Thermoregulation & Vitamin D

Concept

How skin manages heat + makes hormones

  • Cool the body vasodilation
  • Dermal blood vessels dilate, heat radiates
  • Eccrine sweat evaporates, takes heat
  • Warm the body vasoconstriction
  • Dermal blood vessels constrict, heat stays in core
  • Arrector pili contract (limited use in humans)
  • Vitamin D synthesis UV-B
  • Cholesterol precursor in epidermis → cholecalciferol
  • Then liver, then kidney activate it
  • Limited excretion salt, urea, water
Takeaway: skin is a heat exchanger. The dermal vascular plexus shunts blood toward or away from the surface depending on what the hypothalamus demands.
06

Clinical

Burns, Wound Healing, Cancer

Draw

When skin fails

  • Burns: 1st (epidermis only), 2nd (partial-thickness, into dermis), 3rd (full-thickness, into hypodermis)
  • Rule of 9s for surface area
  • Wound healing stages: hemostasis, inflammation, proliferation, remodeling
  • Scar = dense irregular CT replacing original architecture
  • Skin cancers:
  • BCC basal cell carcinoma, most common, least aggressive
  • SCC squamous cell carcinoma
  • Melanoma from melanocytes, most aggressive (ABCDE)
Draw: a body outline. Use the rule of 9s to label percentages on each region (head 9, each arm 9, each leg 18, torso front 18, torso back 18, perineum 1).
