01

Definition

The Integumentary System

Concept

An organ made of multiple tissues

  • Largest organ in the body
  • Multiple tissues acting as one structure
  • Deeper layers are vascular + innervated
  • Outer layer is avascular
  • Skin = epidermis + dermis
  • Hypodermis is associated but not part of skin proper
Takeaway: the integumentary system isn't just skin. It's skin + accessory structures (hair, nails, glands). The next deck handles accessories; this one is layers only.
02

Epidermis

The Five Strata

Reference

Deep to superficial

  • Basale germinativum
  • Stem cells, melanocytes, Merkel cells
  • Spinosum spiny
  • Keratin synthesis begins, Langerhans cells
  • Granulosum grainy
  • Lamellar granules, waterproof barrier forms
  • Lucidum thick skin only
  • Palms, soles, fingertips
  • Corneum dead, shed
Mnemonic: "Bad Skin Gets Lupus Catastrophically" (basale, spinosum, granulosum, lucidum, corneum). Cells flatten, lose nuclei, and die as they move up.

03

Cells

Epidermal Cell Types

Draw

Four cells, four jobs

  • Keratinocyte 95%+
  • Make keratin; main structural cell
  • Melanocyte basale
  • Make melanin; UV protection
  • Merkel cell basale
  • Touch receptor
  • Langerhans cell spinosum
  • Dendritic immune cell
Draw: a wedge of stratum basale and spinosum. Place a keratinocyte, melanocyte (with finger-like extensions), Merkel cell (with nerve fiber), and Langerhans cell. Label each.
04

Dermis

Papillary & Reticular Layers

Concept

Two sublayers of dense CT

  • Papillary layer superficial
  • Loose areolar CT
  • Dermal papillae (fingerprints!)
  • Meissner corpuscles (light touch)
  • Reticular layer deep
  • Dense irregular CT
  • Thick collagen + elastin mesh
  • Pacinian corpuscles (deep pressure)
Takeaway: the dermis is where the work happens. Blood vessels, nerves, glands, hair follicles, sensory receptors. The epidermis is just the protective wrapper.

06

Color + clinical

Pigmentation & Disorders

Concept

Pigments + disorders

  • Melanin eumelanin/pheomelanin
  • Made by melanocytes, transferred via melanosomes
  • Carotene (yellow), hemoglobin (pink)
  • Albinism no melanin
  • Vitiligo patchy loss
  • Jaundice bilirubin
  • Cyanosis low O2
Takeaway: color reveals systemic state. Yellow = liver. Blue = oxygenation. Bronze = adrenal. Pale = anemia or shock. Skin is the body's display screen.
