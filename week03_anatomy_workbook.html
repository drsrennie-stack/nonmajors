Anatomy · Drawing from Memory

Action potential and ANS organ effects

Without referencing figures above, draw two diagrams. Label every component.

Drawing 1: Action potential graph

Plot membrane potential (mV) vs time (ms) showing all five phases of an action potential: resting potential, depolarization, repolarization, hyperpolarization, return to rest. Label which ion channels open and close at each phase (Na⁺ voltage-gated, K⁺ voltage-gated). Mark the threshold value (typically -55 mV) and the peak (typically +30 mV). Indicate the absolute and relative refractory periods.

Drawing space: action potential graph

Drawing 2: ANS effects on three organs

For three organs (heart, lungs, GI tract) draw a simple diagram showing the effect of sympathetic activation (left side) and parasympathetic activation (right side). For each effect, write the receptor type involved (alpha-1, beta-1, beta-2, M3) and what the effect is in plain English.

Drawing space: ANS effects on heart, lungs, GI tract
Synthesis question

A patient with severe asthma is given epinephrine. Trace what happens at the receptor level in the lungs and the heart. Explain why epinephrine relieves the asthma but also causes the patient's heart to race. Reference specific receptor subtypes (alpha-1, beta-1, beta-2) from your drawing above.
