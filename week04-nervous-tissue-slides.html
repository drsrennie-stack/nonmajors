The working cell

Parts of a neuron

Dendrites
Branching receivers; bring the signal toward the soma.
Soma (cell body)
Nucleus and most organelles; the integration point.
Axon
Sends the signal away from the soma; can be very long.
Axon terminal
Releases neurotransmitter at the synapse.
Myelin sheath
Fatty insulation on the axon; speeds conduction.
Nodes of Ranvier
Gaps between myelin; the site of fast saltatory conduction.

Sorted two ways

Classes of neurons

Multipolar

Many dendrites, one axon. Most neurons.

Bipolar

One dendrite, one axon. Retina, olfactory.

Pseudounipolar

One process that splits. Most sensory neurons.

Sensory (afferent)

Carries the signal toward the CNS.

Motor (efferent)

Carries the signal from the CNS to an effector.

Interneuron

Sits between sensory and motor, entirely in the CNS. About 99% of neurons.

The support cells

Neuroglia

Glia outnumber neurons and do the housekeeping. Four types in the CNS, two in the PNS.

Astrocyte

Most numerous; blood-brain barrier and K+ buffering.

Oligodendrocyte

Myelinates CNS axons; one cell wraps several.

Microglia

CNS macrophages; immune surveillance.

Ependymal

Lines the ventricles; makes and circulates CSF.

Schwann cell

Myelinates PNS axons; one cell wraps one segment.

Satellite cell

Surrounds cell bodies in ganglia.

The big four

Major brain regions

Cerebrum
Largest region. Cognition, sensation, and voluntary movement.
Diencephalon
Thalamus, hypothalamus, and epithalamus.
Brainstem
Midbrain, pons, and medulla. Vital reflexes.
Cerebellum
Coordination, balance, and motor learning.

Map of the cortex

Cerebral lobes

Frontal
Voluntary motor (precentral gyrus), executive function, personality, Broca speech.
Parietal
Somatosensation (postcentral gyrus) and spatial orientation.
Temporal
Hearing, language comprehension (Wernicke), memory.
Occipital
Vision.
Insula
Deep lobe; taste and interoception.

Relay, regulate, and keep you alive

Diencephalon and brainstem

Thalamus

Sensory relay station for everything except smell.

Hypothalamus

Homeostasis: temperature, hunger, thirst, hormones via the pituitary.

Pineal gland

Epithalamus; melatonin and circadian rhythm.

Midbrain

Visual and auditory reflexes; substantia nigra.

Pons

Bridge between cerebrum and cerebellum; respiratory control.

Medulla

Cardiac, vasomotor, and respiratory centers; decussation of the pyramids.

The cable and its cross-section

Spinal cord

Extent

From the medulla to L1/L2 in adults. 31 pairs of spinal nerves exit through the intervertebral foramina.

Landmarks

Cervical and lumbar enlargements serve the limbs; the conus medullaris is the tapered end; the cauda equina is the nerve roots below it.

Gray matter (H-shape)

Dorsal horn takes sensory input; ventral horn holds motor neuron cell bodies; lateral horn (T1 to L2) holds sympathetic neurons.

White matter

Ascending and descending tracts in columns; central canal carries CSF.

Protection and plumbing

Meninges, CSF, and the barrier

Meninges (out to in)

Dura mater (tough outer), arachnoid mater (web-like middle; subarachnoid space holds CSF), pia mater (thin, follows every contour).

Blood-brain barrier

Tight junctions between brain capillary cells plus astrocyte feet. Lets in lipid-soluble molecules and glucose; excludes most drugs and toxins.

CSF flow: choroid plexus → lateral ventricles → third ventricle → cerebral aqueduct → fourth ventricle → subarachnoid space → arachnoid villi → venous blood. It cushions, gives buoyancy, and stabilizes the chemistry.
