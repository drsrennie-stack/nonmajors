How this workbook works

Print, handwrite, scan, submit. Complete every section. Drawings, labeling, tables, and clinical reasoning all earn points.

About the drawings in this workbook. For each drawing, find a clear reference in OpenStax Anatomy and Physiology 2e or any anatomy atlas. You may either (a) draw it by hand in the workbook box, or (b) build it digitally in Google Docs, Google Drawings, Word, or PowerPoint using shapes, lines, and text labels, then attach the file to your Canvas submission.

If you build digitally, you must submit revision history showing you built the diagram in stages over time. In Google Docs and Google Drawings: File → Version history → See version history. A flat image with no revision history does not meet this requirement.

Either way, you must build it yourself, structure by structure. Copying a finished image (from Google Images, an AI tool, or a peer) earns zero on the assignment and is reported as academic dishonesty. Submission to Canvas certifies the work as your own.

Anatomy · Drawing from Reference

Visual pathway and pupillary light reflex

Without referencing figures above, draw two pathways. Label every component.

Drawing 1: Visual pathway from retina to occipital cortex

Draw the visual pathway from both eyes through to the visual cortex. Show what happens at the optic chiasm. Required components: both retinas (label nasal and temporal halves), optic nerve (CN II), optic chiasm with crossover of nasal fibers, optic tract, lateral geniculate nucleus of thalamus, optic radiation, primary visual cortex (occipital lobe).

Drawing space

Drawing 2: Pupillary light reflex pathway

Shine a light into one eye and both pupils constrict (direct + consensual response). Draw the reflex arc. Required components: retina (sensory input), CN II, pretectal nucleus (midbrain), Edinger-Westphal nucleus (bilateral), CN III (parasympathetic motor output), ciliary ganglion, sphincter pupillae muscle (both eyes). Mark which side is afferent and which is efferent.

Drawing space
Synthesis question

A patient comes to the ER after a head injury. You shine a light in the right eye and both pupils constrict normally. You shine a light in the left eye and neither pupil constricts. Which cranial nerve is damaged on which side, and how does the reflex pathway above explain the asymmetric finding?
