Your prompt

What to write about.

This week we covered blood, the heart, the conduction system, and blood vessels. Blood pressure is the integrated readout of all of them.

For your initial post, work through the following scenario. (250–350 words.)

A 68-year-old patient walks into clinic. BP today is 158/94. Heart rate 76, regular. They report feeling fine. They are on no medications. They are about 30 lbs above their ideal weight.
  1. What does each number mean? (systolic, diastolic, units, what physiologic event each captures)
  2. Which compartments of the cardiovascular system are contributing to elevated pressure?
  3. If you could change ONE thing about this patient's physiology to lower BP first, what would you pick and why?
  4. What would you want to ask the patient before making any recommendation?
  5. Now make it personal. Run the cardiovascular tools below (use your own numbers if you know them, otherwise the patient's). Say what they told you about your own cardiovascular health — no need to share exact numbers — then pick one change that would lower the risk and explain its mechanism of action: physiologically, how does it lower blood pressure or 10-year risk (think vascular resistance, blood volume, or LDL and arterial plaque)?

BP category, pulse pressure, MAP

AHA 2017 categories. Pulse pressure = SBP − DBP. MAP ≈ DBP + ⅓(PP).

Target heart rate (Karvonen)

Max HR = 220 − age. Heart rate reserve = Max − Resting. Zones scale from there.

ASCVD 10-year risk (Pooled Cohort Equations)

2013 ACC/AHA Pooled Cohort Equations. Valid for ages 40 to 79 without prior ASCVD. Used in clinical practice to guide statin decisions. Total cholesterol and HDL in mg/dL.
