## Answer Key

1. **C** — Plasma is ~55% of whole blood; red cells are ~45% (the hematocrit).
2. **B** — Erythrocytes (red blood cells) carry O₂ on hemoglobin.
3. **C** — Blood is tightly regulated at pH 7.35–7.45.
4. **B** — Hemopoiesis is blood-cell formation; hemostasis is stopping bleeding.
5. **B** — Vascular spasm, then platelet plug, then coagulation (fibrin clot).
6. **D** — Type O red cells have neither A nor B antigens.
7. **B** — O-negative red cells lack A, B, and Rh(D) antigens, so they can be given broadly.
8. **B** — The myocardium is the contractile muscle layer.
9. **B** — Pericardial fluid lubricates and reduces friction (and limits overfilling).
10. **C** — The right AV (tricuspid) valve lies between the right atrium and right ventricle.
11. **B** — The left ventricle pumps into the aorta (systemic circuit).
12. **B** — The SA node is the primary pacemaker (~80–100 bpm intrinsic rate).
13. **B** — The P wave = atrial depolarization; QRS = ventricular depolarization; T = ventricular repolarization.
14. **B** — Cardiac output = heart rate × stroke volume.
15. **B** — The tunica media holds smooth muscle and elastic fibers; it controls vessel diameter.
16. **C** — Capillaries have thin walls for exchange.
17. **B** — Pulmonary arteries carry deoxygenated blood to the lungs (pulmonary veins carry oxygen-rich blood back).
18. **B** — ~85% of filtered fluid is reabsorbed; ~15% is returned via the lymphatic system.
19. **A** — MAP ≈ cardiac output × total peripheral resistance.
20. **C** — 140 systolic and/or 90 diastolic is the common hypertension threshold used in the lecture.
