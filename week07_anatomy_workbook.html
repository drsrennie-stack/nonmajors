How this workbook works

Print, handwrite, scan, submit. Complete every section. Drawings, labeling, tables, and clinical reasoning all earn points.

About the drawings in this workbook. For each drawing, find a clear reference in OpenStax Anatomy and Physiology 2e or any anatomy atlas. You may either (a) draw it by hand in the workbook box, or (b) build it digitally in Google Docs, Google Drawings, Word, or PowerPoint using shapes, lines, and text labels, then attach the file to your Canvas submission.

If you build digitally, you must submit revision history showing you built the diagram in stages over time. In Google Docs and Google Drawings: File → Version history → See version history. A flat image with no revision history does not meet this requirement.

Either way, you must build it yourself, structure by structure. Copying a finished image (from Google Images, an AI tool, or a peer) earns zero on the assignment and is reported as academic dishonesty. Submission to Canvas certifies the work as your own.

Anatomy · Drawing from Reference

Hepatic lobule blood flow and digestive enzyme map

Find each diagram in OpenStax Anatomy and Physiology 2e or any anatomy atlas. Reproduce each diagram in the box and label every required component.

Drawing 1: Blood flow through a hepatic lobule

Draw a hexagonal hepatic lobule with all six portal triads at corners and the central vein in the middle. Use arrows to show: blood flowing IN from the hepatic artery and portal vein at the portal triads, through sinusoids past hepatocytes, OUT into the central vein. Required components: portal triad (label all three vessels: hepatic artery branch, portal vein branch, bile ductule), hepatic sinusoids, hepatocytes in plates, Kupffer cells, central vein, bile canaliculi (with arrows showing bile flow direction, opposite to blood flow).

Drawing space

Drawing 2: Digestive enzyme map

Draw a simplified GI tract from mouth to small intestine. At each major site, label the enzyme(s) released, the source, and the substrate they digest. Required entries: mouth (salivary amylase, salivary glands, starch), stomach (pepsinogen → pepsin, chief cells with parietal cell HCl, proteins), small intestine - pancreatic contributions (pancreatic amylase, lipase, trypsin/chymotrypsin, with substrates), small intestine - brush border (lactase, sucrase, maltase, peptidases, with substrates).

Drawing space
Synthesis question

A patient with cirrhosis has scarred liver tissue that disrupts normal lobular architecture. Using your hepatic lobule drawing, predict what happens to (1) blood flow through the liver, (2) bile drainage, and (3) the liver's ability to detoxify substances absorbed from the gut.
