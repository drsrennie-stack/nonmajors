Your prompt

What to write about.

This week we covered the lymphatic and immune systems, the respiratory system, and the digestive system. Each is a barrier between your body and the outside world.

Pick one situation where one of these barriers was breached: illness, infection, allergy, food poisoning, asthma attack, choking incident, anything where the line between "outside" and "inside" got crossed.

In your initial post (200-300 words):

  1. Describe the situation briefly.
  2. Identify the barrier that failed (anatomically and at the tissue level).
  3. Trace the body's response: what innate defenses fired first? Did the adaptive immune system get involved? How?
  4. What healed it, and how would you predict the body would respond to the same insult next time?

Optional: try the tools clinicians use

Respiratory screening tools.

Two screening questionnaires used in clinic for sleep apnea risk and asthma control. Try them with your own answers, or work through a scenario from a classmate or a clinical case you've seen.

If you've been screened or treated for either, you can plug in your own answers for a more authentic exercise. Not required. Nothing is saved on this device. Nothing is shared. Refresh the page to clear everything.

STOP-BANG sleep apnea screen

Eight yes/no items. Used as a pre-anesthesia and primary care screening tool for obstructive sleep apnea.

Asthma Control Test (ACT)

Five questions, 4 weeks recall. Used in primary care and pulmonology to track asthma management.

In the past 4 weeks, how much of the time did asthma keep you from work, school, or home activities?

How often have you had shortness of breath?

How often did asthma symptoms wake you up at night or earlier than usual?

How often did you use a rescue inhaler (e.g., albuterol)?

How would you rate your asthma control during the past 4 weeks?
