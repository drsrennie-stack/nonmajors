How this workbook works

Print, handwrite, scan, submit. Complete every section. Drawings, labeling, tables, and clinical reasoning all earn points.

About the drawings in this workbook. For each drawing, find a clear reference in OpenStax Anatomy and Physiology 2e or any anatomy atlas. You may either (a) draw it by hand in the workbook box, or (b) build it digitally in Google Docs, Google Drawings, Word, or PowerPoint using shapes, lines, and text labels, then attach the file to your Canvas submission.

If you build digitally, you must submit revision history showing you built the diagram in stages over time. In Google Docs and Google Drawings: File → Version history → See version history. A flat image with no revision history does not meet this requirement.

Either way, you must build it yourself, structure by structure. Copying a finished image (from Google Images, an AI tool, or a peer) earns zero on the assignment and is reported as academic dishonesty. Submission to Canvas certifies the work as your own.

Capstone integration: HPG axis and fetal-to-newborn circulation transition

Find each diagram in OpenStax Anatomy and Physiology 2e or any anatomy atlas. Reproduce each integrated diagram in the box and label every required component.

Drawing 1: Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Gonadal (HPG) axis

Draw the full HPG axis for either male or female (your choice). Required components: hypothalamus releasing GnRH, anterior pituitary releasing FSH and LH, gonad (testis or ovary) producing sex hormones, target tissues, AND negative feedback arrows from sex hormones back to hypothalamus and pituitary. If you choose female, include positive feedback at mid-cycle (LH surge).

Drawing space

Drawing 2: Fetal-to-newborn circulation transition

Draw two side-by-side hearts: fetal circulation (with all three shunts open and umbilical vessels active) and newborn circulation (after first breath). Show what closes, what opens, and why. Required components: foramen ovale, ductus arteriosus, ductus venosus, umbilical vessels, pulmonary circuit (with arrows showing blood flow direction in each scenario). For the newborn, indicate why pulmonary vascular resistance drops and how that drives the foramen ovale to close.
