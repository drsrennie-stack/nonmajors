Your prompt

What to write about.

Last week of the course. This is a synthesis discussion.

Pick one body system you came in already familiar with and one body system you came in knowing little about.

In your initial post (350–450 words):

  1. For the familiar system: what's one concept you now understand differently than you did on day one? Be specific.
  2. For the unfamiliar system: what's one moment from this term where the lightbulb went on?
  3. Across the whole course: pick one connection between two systems that you didn't expect when you started. (Examples: the connection between kidney filtration and blood pressure; between the nervous system and the muscle action potential; between digestion and immunity.)
  4. Forward look: what's one question about the human body you'd want to chase next?
  5. Now make it personal. Run the renal and fluid-balance tools below. Say what they told you about your own kidney and hydration health — no need to share exact numbers — then pick one change that would protect kidney function and explain its mechanism of action: physiologically, how does it help (think supporting filtration and GFR, protecting the nephrons, or controlling blood pressure and glucose)?

Step 5 of your post · try the clinical tools

Renal, fluid, and reproductive calculators.

Three quick tools tied to this week's material. Work them with the defaults, or plug in your own numbers if you have them.

If you've had recent labs or are tracking a pregnancy, you can plug in your own numbers for a more authentic exercise. Not required. Nothing is saved on this device. Nothing is shared. Refresh the page to clear everything.

Creatinine clearance (Cockcroft-Gault)

CrCl = ((140 − age) × weight) / (72 × sCr), × 0.85 for female. Used to estimate kidney function for drug dosing.

Daily water needs (baseline)

Rough baseline: ~30 to 35 mL per kg of body weight per day. Increase for heat, exertion, illness, lactation.

Pregnancy due date (Naegele's Rule)

EDD = first day of last menstrual period + 280 days. Assumes a 28-day cycle with ovulation on day 14.
