Calc · APGAR & Naegele's Rule Lab

Newborn scoring and pregnancy dating

Lab anchor. Show your work.

APGAR scoring: A (color), P (pulse), G (grimace/reflex), A (activity/tone), R (respiration). Each scored 0/1/2. Total 0-10.
Naegele's Rule: EDD = LMP + 7 days − 3 months + 1 year (= LMP + 280 days)

Apply

Apply task. a. A patient is found dead in their home approximately 6 hours after a witnessed cardiac arrest. The body was found face-down on the bedroom floor. Predict where livor mortis would be visible, whether rigor mortis would be present, and what the body temperature would be relative to ambient room temperature. Explain each prediction.

Apply

Apply task. b. A patient is unresponsive 48 hours after a severe traumatic brain injury, on a ventilator. The neurologist documents fixed and dilated pupils, absent corneal reflex, absent oculocephalic response, and absent gag reflex. Identify the next required test before brain death can be declared, identify two confounders that would invalidate the brain death exam, and explain why hypothermia must be ruled out first.

Apply

Apply task. c. A family asks how their loved one can still have a heartbeat if the doctor has declared them brain dead. Explain in plain language: the difference between cardiopulmonary death and brain death, the role of the brainstem in spontaneous breathing, and why mechanical ventilation can sustain heart function temporarily even after the brain has died.
