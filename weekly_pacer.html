A typical week in this course.

Day-by-day rhythm with realistic time estimates. Use this template every week to stay on pace in the 8-week intensive.

Why pacing matters in this course

This course covers in 8 weeks what a standard semester course covers in 16. You will need 14 to 18 hours per week for course work outside of synchronous time. Falling one week behind in this format is roughly equivalent to falling two weeks behind in a regular semester.

This pacer is the rhythm of students who succeed. Adjust to your schedule, but try not to compress more than 2 days into a single push.

Monday
Set the foundation
2hours
  • VideosWatch concept lectures for the week
    All concept videos for the week, in order. Take rough notes. Do not try to memorize.
  • VideosWatch lab ID videos
    Identify structures you will see on lab exams. Pause and rewind freely.
  • TrainerFirst pass through the trainer
    Get oriented to the layout. Don't try to master everything yet.
Tuesday

2.5hours
  • TrainerSecond trainer session
    Now actively retrieve. Try to answer before checking. Track which sections feel weakest.
  • ReadingOpenStax reading for any weak sections
    Re-watch concept videos for sections that felt weak in the trainer.
  • ProjectSelf-experimentation journal (Weeks 1, 2, 4, 5, 7)
    Open the weekly journal form. Begin tracking time data and confidence ratings as the week progresses.
Wednesday
Engage and discuss
2hours
  • DiscussionPost initial discussion response
    Initial post due Wednesday 11:59 PM. Substantive, evidence-based, your own words.
  • TrainerThird trainer session
    Focus on weak areas identified Tuesday. Repetition matters more than length.
  • ProjectExperiment design (Weeks 3 and 6 only)
    Submit experiment design by end of day if it is Week 3 or Week 6.
Thursday
Anatomy workbook
3.5hours
  • AnatomyPrint the anatomy workbook
    Have it printed before you start. Handwriting matters. Have pen, pencil, and ruler ready.
  • AnatomyComplete labeling exercises
    Label every numbered structure on every figure. If a label blank is empty, leave it blank rather than guessing.
  • AnatomyComplete drawings from memory
    Heavily weighted. Draw structures by hand without referencing the figures. Label every component.
  • AnatomyAnswer synthesis questions
    Heavily weighted. Use evidence from the anatomy you drew. AI-resistant by design.
Friday
Physiology workbook
3hours
  • PhysiologyPrint the physiology workbook
    Read the introduction and the reference boxes before starting calculations.
  • PhysiologyWork through lab skill sections
    Calculations, data tables, lab interpretation. Show your work in the work boxes provided.
  • PhysiologyComplete clinical cases
    3–5 patient cases per week. Apply lab skills to specific patients. Reference anatomy from the partner anatomy workbook.
  • PhysiologyFinal synthesis question
    Most heavily weighted question of the week. Integrates 4–6 specific elements named in the prompt.
Saturday
Review and reply
2hours
  • DiscussionReply to two peers
    Substantive replies that build on or respectfully challenge their thinking. Cite evidence.
  • TrainerFinal trainer session as quiz prep
    Focus on the sections that gave you trouble in the workbook. Strengthen weak spots.
  • WorkbooksScan and review your workbooks
    Make sure every section is complete. Scan to PDF. Save filenames as LastName_Week_N_Anatomy.pdf and LastName_Week_N_Physiology.pdf.
Sunday
Submit and finish
1.5hours
  • WorkbooksSubmit both workbooks to Canvas
    Anatomy and Physiology workbooks both due 11:59 PM. Upload to the matching Canvas assignments.

Weekly time budget

Concept and lab videos 2 hrs ~12%
Cognitive trainer (3–4 sessions) 4 hrs ~25%
Anatomy workbook 3.5 hrs ~22%
Physiology workbook 3 hrs ~19%
Discussion (post + 2 replies) 1.5 hrs ~9%
Quiz prep + quiz 1 hr ~6%
Project deliverables (variable) 1 hr ~6%
Total weekly course time ~16 hrs 100%

Do

Habits of students who succeed

  • Block specific hours on your calendar for course work and protect them like work shifts.
  • Run the trainer in 30–45 minute sessions. More sessions, less length each.
  • Print workbooks BEFORE you sit down to do them. Don't break flow to print.
  • Take the quiz fresh, not exhausted. Schedule it earlier on Sunday if possible.
  • Email Dr. Rennie BEFORE missing a deadline if life is hitting hard. Solutions exist before the deadline, not after.
  • Use the Self-Experimentation Project as your built-in feedback loop. Honest journals catch problems early.

Don't

Patterns that hurt students in this format

  • Don't try to do all 16 hours in one weekend. Spaced practice is core to retention.
  • Don't skip the trainer to "save time" before the workbook. The workbook builds on the trainer.
  • Don't leave the quiz for late Sunday night. Honorlock issues happen at the worst times.
  • Don't paste AI text into discussion posts or synthesis answers. They are designed to be detectable.
  • Don't treat the workbook as a fill-in-the-blanks. The drawings and synthesis questions are where points come from.
  • Don't assume "I'll catch up next week." In an 8-week course, falling behind compounds fast.

One last thing

This pacer is a template, not a contract. If you have a baseline that works (early-morning study, weekend study, late-night study), keep it. The hours and the order of operations matter more than the specific days. Whatever schedule you set, write it down somewhere visible.

The students who do best in this course are not the ones who are smartest. They are the ones who built and protected a weekly rhythm. You can do this.
