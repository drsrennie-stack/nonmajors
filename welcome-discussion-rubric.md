# Welcome Discussion Rubric · BIO 304

**Rubric Name:** Welcome Discussion
**Type:** Scale
**Rating Display:** Level
**Rating Order:** High &lt; Low (highest points on the right, matches your screenshot)
**Scoring:** Scored
**Use this rubric for assignment grading:** Yes
**Total: 5 points**

---

## Criterion 1: Introduction completeness (2 points)

**Description for the criterion box:** Your initial post addresses the five prompt items: your name, your education path, one body system you're curious about, one thing about your life outside of school, and what time of day you do your best thinking.

| Rating level (left, lowest) → (right, highest) | Points | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Not yet | 0 | Brief post, or fewer than three of the five prompt items addressed, or no post. |
| Partial | 1 | Touches three or four of the five items, but one or two are missing or one-line. |
| Complete | 2 | Touches all five items with enough detail that a classmate can connect with you. |

---

## Criterion 2: Community-building tone (1 point)

**Description for the criterion box:** Your post sounds like you. It shows your voice, not a generic introduction, and invites a classmate to start a conversation with you.

| Rating level | Points | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Not yet | 0 | Generic, formal-only, or impersonal. Reads like a form, not a person. |
| There | 1 | Genuine, specific, and shows up as a real human classmates can connect with. |

---

## Criterion 3: Replies to two classmates (2 points)

**Description for the criterion box:** You reply to at least two classmates by Sunday. Each reply builds a connection: shared interest, shared time-of-day rhythm, shared path, or a curious question back to them.

| Rating level | Points | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Not yet | 0 | Zero or one reply posted, or replies are "nice intro"-style with no substance. |
| Partial | 1 | Two replies posted, but one is brief or one-line. |
| Both substantive | 2 | Two thoughtful replies to two different classmates, each building a real connection. |

---

## How to enter this in Canvas

1. In the Criteria Builder, click **Draft New Criterion** three times to create three rows.
2. For each row, click into the criterion to edit:
   - Paste the **criterion name** as the title
   - Paste the **description** in the description field
   - Add three rating columns (or two for Criterion 2) with the **point values and descriptions** from the tables above
3. Confirm the total reads **5 Points Possible** at the top right of the builder.
4. Save the rubric. Attach it to the Welcome Discussion topic in its settings.

---

## Optional: shorter 2-criterion version

If you'd rather keep it minimal:

- **Initial post (3 points)**: 0 / 1 (partial) / 2 (meets) / 3 (full and substantive)
- **Two substantive replies (2 points)**: 0 / 1 (one full, one brief) / 2 (both substantive)

Same five total points, fewer rows to grade.
