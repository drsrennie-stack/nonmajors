02 What this course is

Two minutes of orientation

BIO 304 is a 4-credit, 8-week, asynchronous online course that combines human anatomy and physiology. Anatomy is the structure (what is where). Physiology is the function (how it behaves). The two are inseparable in clinical practice and inseparable in this course. Each week you will work through both, in parallel, and connect them.

The course is built for pre-nursing, EMT, paramedic, mortuary science, and preparation for advanced A&P coursework. The content is calibrated for people who will use this material in real clinical decisions, soon.

What a typical week looks like

  • Six hours of video lectures (four hours new, two hours existing concept and lab ID videos).
  • One anatomy workbook and one physiology workbook (graded, structured, draw-from-reference).
  • Three to four cognitive trainer sessions (spaced retrieval practice, ungraded).
  • One clinical portfolio page (the week's anatomy and physiology translated into a clinical reference tool).
  • One Honorlock-proctored quiz, twenty questions, twenty minutes.
  • One discussion post and two replies.

Total student engagement is approximately 20 to 20.5 hours per week. This is what a 4-credit, 8-week intensive looks like. Plan for it.

04 Tech setup checklist

Do these before Week 1, in order

  1. Verify your Canvas access. Log in. Confirm you can see the BIO 304 course shell. If you cannot, contact the ARC help desk through the Canvas help menu before Monday of Week 1.
  2. Install Honorlock. Honorlock is the proctoring tool used for the eight weekly quizzes. The installation is a Chrome extension; install it on a laptop or desktop with a working webcam and microphone. Do this before your first quiz, not five minutes before.
  3. Confirm your file conversion path. Workbooks, portfolio pages, and any handwritten work submitted as PDF must convert cleanly. If you are unsure how to make a PDF on your device, the Submission Directions document has step-by-step guides for iPhone, iPad, Android, Windows, and Mac.
  4. Test your camera and microphone. Honorlock requires both. Run a test recording on your machine to confirm both work.
  5. Pick your study space. Quiet, well-lit, no other people in frame. Honorlock will flag distractions during quizzes.
  6. Gather your supplies. Blank paper, pencils with erasers, colored pencils (helpful for anatomy drawings), a textbook or atlas (your choice; recommendations are in the syllabus).
05 Get help

Where to go when something goes wrong

Course content questions

Post in the weekly discussion forum if your question is one other students might also have. Email me directly for individual situations. I respond within 24 hours on weekdays, 48 hours on weekends.

Virtual office hours

Held weekly via the link in Canvas. Drop in with anything: a confusing topic, a workbook section that isn't clicking, a portfolio page you're stuck on. You don't need an appointment.

Accessibility and accommodations (DSPS)

If you need accommodations, contact ARC Disabled Student Programs and Services (DSPS) early in the term. Email dsps@arc.losrios.edu, phone (916) 484-8382, located at the North Entrance of the Welcome and Support Center Building. All approved accommodations are honored without exception. Once DSPS approves your accommodation letter, share it with me by email so I know to expect it.

Tech support

  • Canvas issues: 24/7 chat and phone support through the help menu inside Canvas.
  • Honorlock issues: Honorlock 24/7 chat support, accessed inside the proctoring window.
  • File conversion issues: Submission Directions document covers most cases; otherwise, email me with the device and the file type.
One last thing

If you're feeling overwhelmed in the first two weeks, reach out. Eight-week intensives compress quickly. The students who come talk to me in Week 2 finish the course. The students who quietly fall behind in Week 2, often don't. I would much rather hear from you early.
