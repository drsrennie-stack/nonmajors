Part 2 of 3 · Anatomy lab

Draw and label

Box A. Hierarchy (whole muscle to myofibril)

Directions

  1. Draw a long oval representing a whole muscle (cross-section). Wrap it in a thin line. Label that line Epimysium.
  2. Inside the oval, draw three or four smaller circles. Each is a fascicle. Wrap one of them with a thin line. Label that line Perimysium.
  3. Inside one fascicle, draw several smaller circles. Each is a muscle fiber (cell). Wrap one with a thin line. Label that line Endomysium.
  4. Inside one muscle fiber, draw a stack of long rods. Each rod is a myofibril. Label one Myofibril.
  5. Above your hierarchy, write the order of wrappings from outside to inside in one short sentence.

Box B. Sarcomere close-up

Directions

  1. Draw a long rectangle. Mark the left and right ends with vertical lines. Label both lines Z line.
  2. In the center, draw a vertical line. Label it M line.
  3. Between the two Z lines, draw thick filaments (myosin) in the middle and thin filaments (actin) extending from each Z line.
  4. Bracket and label the A band (the full length of the thick filaments).
  5. Bracket and label the I band (the region with only thin filaments).
  6. Bracket and label the H zone (the central thick-only region).
  7. Draw a T-tubule entering from above and a sarcoplasmic reticulum wrapping the myofibril. Label both.

A. Mechanism trace: from action potential to power stroke

B. Synthesis

Answer each in two to four sentences, using this week's vocabulary and your drawings as evidence.

1. The I band shortens dramatically during contraction, but the A band barely changes length. Explain why, in terms of which filaments make up each band.
2. A toxin disrupts the triad junctions specifically (the points where T-tubules meet the SR). Predict the effect on contraction and explain at which step the cycle fails.
3. A muscle is stretched so far that thick and thin filaments no longer overlap. Predict the tension the muscle can generate at this length, and justify your answer using the cross-bridge mechanism.
